OTP AWARDS

One of the OTP traditions that Pioneer Mike Gessner established in the early days of the group is the process of presenting awards to deserving hikers at the yearly Anniversary Party. These awards are meant to highlight or recognize certain events that occurred during the hikes of the past year.Â  They are always given in the spirit of good fun, usually at the expense of the hiker receiving the award.

The awards are given under the direction of the Honors and Award Committee, a group of fellow hikers that note awardable events occurring during the year. The Committee actively encourages other hikers to report qualifying events to the Committee. Phil Rutherford has agreed to forward any such reports to the Committee.

The following lists the awards that have been presented before and a description of the pre-requisites for receiving them. The Committee is quite capable of creating new awards to fit the circumstance should an event fall beyond the existing criteria.

Pathfinder Award - issued to any leader who gets lost leading their own hike.

Sally Forth Award - awarded to any hiker who cannot follow the leader and gets lost on the hike (named in honor of Sally Spotts, whose hiking experiences were the genesis of this award).

Order of the Purple Heart Award - awarded to any hiker who accidentally gets hurt or becomes ill on the hike.

Bad Luck Award - awarded to any hiker that loses anything on the hike, leaves their hiking pole at the lunch spot, forgets their lunch or their boots, etc.

Horologist Award - awarded to any leader who is more than 15 minutes late to their hike (unless they have called ahead explaining their delay).

Cookie Monster Award - awarded to leader who neglects to bring cookies or snacks.

Lost Sheep Award - awarded to the hiker who gets lost on a hike to the point where professional help is required to rescue the hiker.

New Hike Recognition Award - awarded to a Leader who successfully searches out and leads the group on a new hiking experience (not modifications to existing hikes). Establishing new hikes is strongly encouraged by the Committee.

Good Sam Award - awarded to the hiker that exemplifies the Law of the Trail by coming to the aid of a hiker in distress.

Death March Award - awarded to the leader who leads a hike that exceeds what the group feels is a reasonable hiking experience.

Productivity Award - awarded to any hiker who earns more awards on a given year than any other hiker in that year.

Perfect Attendance Award - awarded to the hiker who attended every hike in the calendar year.

Special Recognition awards given as appropriate to recognize a hiker’s special contribution to the Society.
