#914


Silverlake Stairs

Distance: 4 miles RT
Elevation Gain: 300 ft.

Summary: The Silverlake neighborhood east of Echo Park varies from quite modest, small homes, weed strewn empty lots, small stores along Sunset Blvd. to large mansions overlooking the lake itself. If all goes well, we will traverse 13 separate stairways containing a total of 1,357 stairs -- some of them going up, some going down. We start and end at the "Music Box Stairs" made famous by the Laurel and Hardy movie depicting them trying to deliver a piano up the stairs. Pianos will not be provided on this hike, so if you are so motivated, bring along your own.

Directions to Trailhead: We meet at the bottom of the Music Box stairs located near 921/923 Vendome Street. Exit the Hollywood (101) freeway at Silver Lake Blvd. and head north. Our goal is to reach West Sunset Blvd. but Silver Lake crosses under Sunset so you either have to take a left before the Sunset bridge onto Parkman Ave, or else a right on Ellett Place. Either one will get you up to Sunset, where you will turn left to go north west.

Look for North Vendome Street on your left just after you cross North Reno St., turn left and park on the street. The Music Box Steps are on the west side of Vendome, and there is an identifying sign on a post at the foot of the stairs, and a plaque on one of the steps.

The Hike: The hike can only be described in tabular form. Each stairway is numbered (i.e. S1, S2...) the direction is given next, the number of stairs and a cum number of stairs is next and then GPS coordinates given for the stariway threshold. End of hike gathering for those who survive will be at the Echo Park park picnic tables. Turn right on West Sunset Blvd. and go to Glendale Blvd, turn left and after a short block, turn left again onto Park Ave. and park.

Going home (from Echo Park park) access to the 101 freeway south is via Glendale Blvd. (turn left off of Park Ave.), and for those going north, turn right and continue down Echo Park Blvd. (the east boundary of the park) to the on-ramp beyond the intersection with Bellevue Ave.
