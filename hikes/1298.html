Hike #1298
November 2, 2016
Smith Mountain via Bear Creek Trail

Dorothy Ferrington, Hike Leader

GPS Data:
To the Saddle: 6 mi. RT, elevation gain = 1145 ft.
Saddle to Peak: 0.6 mi. one way, elevation gain = 840 ft.
Total to Peak: 7.2 mi. RT; elevation gain = 1985 ft.

Directions to Trailhead: Drive up Hwy 39 from Azusa, 17 miles, to the beginning of the Bear Creek trail, marked by a metal sign; 1/4 mile before Coldbrook Camp (Paddle 32.12). Park on the left side of the road.

The Hike: Walk 3 miles up the well graded Bear Creek trail to Smith Saddle at the top of the ridge. Those with lots of energy can do an optional 1/2 mile additional steep scramble directly up the ridge to the top of Smith Mountain following traces of a path beaten by climbers. Watch your footing during the ridge descent.

Lunch at the saddle (or peak), then return to the trailhead for refreshments. Note that there are no places for seating - please bring chairs.
