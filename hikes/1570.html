Hike #1570
Altadena Fire Roads and El Prieto Canyon
May 15, 2024

Hike Leader:  Rick Martin

Meet at 8:00 am for start at 8:30 am
Rabbits will start at an earlier time to be determined.

Please print your own hike descriptions and maps.

Overview:   The hike is a total of 6.2 miles on fire roads and a final leg up El Prieto Canyon. It is totally within the Angeles National Forest just North of Altadena. A map (which is provided below) and following directions are essential. Shorter and longer variations are available.

Trailhead:  Exit the 210 freeway on Lincoln Ave. in North/West Pasadena. Proceed North on Lincoln. There are no restrooms at the trailhead but you will be passing a McDonalds at 2157 Lincoln Ave. Notice the Altadena Community Garden and Loma Alta Park at about 1.8 miles from the freeway (this is where the post hike party will be held). Continue up Lincoln another block and turn left on Canyon Crest Road. Follow the (very) curvy Canyon Crest Road to its junction with Rising Hill Road.  Turn left on Rising Hill and right on El Prieto Road to address 4552. Park here in the residential area. Be courteous and keep quiet.

Hike:  Start hiking at the black gate at the end of the road.  Follow the fire road to a flat spot.  Then take the trail to the lrft towards the Brown Gravesite.  There is an interesting side trip on the right to the gravesite on Little Round Top.  Return to the trail and proceed up the fire road 0.25 miles to a junction with the road that comes up from Millard Camp. Go left (away from Millard), and the road reaches into El Prieto Canyon and crosses the small stream. About 150 yards past the stream, note, but do not take, the signed El Prieto Trail that drops into the canyon.

Continue up the road to the major junction with a road/trail that leads north to Oakwilde (2380 ft.). Stay left at the junction and follow the road (2N66) down for 3.1 miles until it drops into El Prieto Canyon (1280 ft.). An obvious sign to the left directs us 2 miles up the canyon, toward the upper junction with the fire road.

There are numerous side trails in the canyon, always stay on the best path near the stream. After about 1.4 miles of climbing up the canyon you will be on a stretch that is away from the stream. A sharp bend to the left, just below a stand of cactus will be encountered. The bend is also a junction with an upward leading trail. Take the upward trail a couple of hundred yards back to the fire road with the yellow gate.  Turn right and return to the cars following the trails and fire road we can up.

Variations:   A shorter hike with a bit of a loop may be obtained by starting as above and turning back at any time. The mini-loop feature is obtained if one drops into El Prieto Canyon at the sign above the stream crossing. Look for the oak to find the trail back to the cars.

Rabbits may add another mile and a couple of hundred feet by ignoring the oak and continuing up the canyon trail until the upper fire road is reached. Then come back down the road to the cars.

Party:  Loma Alta Park at 3330 Lincoln Ave, Altadena, about 1.9 miles back down the hill. Enter the park from the South on Palm Street.
