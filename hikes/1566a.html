Brown Mountain Summit

Please print your own hike descriptions and maps. 

Directions to Trailhead:  From the 210 Freeway drive north on Angeles Crest Hwy (Hwy 2) for 17.4 miles to the Red Box Picnic Area on the right (south) side of the road. From Red Box take the Mount Wilson Road south for 2.3 miles to unsigned Eaton Saddle and park. Remember to display your National Parks Pass on your dashboard.

Hike Summary:  Eaton Saddle Trailhead to Brown Mountain summit, ~9 miles RT, cum. elevation gain = 2,022 ft.

Map Legend:
Basic Hike - Pink
Option 1 – Blue
Option 2 – Green
Bonus Peak - Yellow

Basic Hike - Brown Mountain Summit (Pink Trail):  From Eaton Saddle, head up the fire road through the Mueller Tunnel, to the unsigned Markham Saddle.  Bypass the San Gabriel Peak trailhead on the right, and the Mt. Lowe East Trail to the left. Continue hiking on the Mount Lowe Motorway (generally to the west). Bypass the junction where the trail makes a sharp 180 degree turn to the left (blue trail on hike map). Do not take this trail. Instead, continue west on the Bear Canyon Trail leading to the Tom Sloan Saddle. Arrive at the Tom Sloane Saddle and the intersection of the Bear Canyon Trail and Dawn Mine Trail (5-way trail intersection). Take the second on the right, unmarked trail, to the left of the Tom Sloane Saddle sign. Follow the trail to the Brown Mountain summit as it meanders up and down along a ridge with four false summits. The fifth summit is Brown Mountain. Take a break and enjoy the views. Return on the same trail/fire road to Eaton Saddle.

Hike Options:

Option 1. Mt Lowe (loop and summit):   Refer to Hike #1533. Blue on hike map below. May be hiked in clockwise or counterclockwise (recommended).

Option 2. Inspiration Point:  Green on hike map below connecting to Mt. Lowe loop route.

Bonus Peak - San Gabriel Peak:  For those that want a shorter hike or want to bag an additional peak. The San Gabriel Peak trail is located near the trailhead on the right (north). Partially noted on the hike map in yellow.

Note: All options can be mixed and matched to create shorter or longer/easier or harder hikes.
