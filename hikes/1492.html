Hike #1492
June 29, 2022
Bridge to Nowhere

Hike Leader:  Randy Sanchez

Meet at 7:30 am for start at 8:00 am.  Note time change.

Please print your own hike descriptions and maps.

Trailhead Directions:  Enter Bridge to Nowhere Trailhead into Google Maps or Apple Maps apps.  Take the I-210 Freeway to the Azusa off ramp.  Turn left (north), Azusa Avenue will become San Gabriel Canyon Road and is also SR 39.  Follow it up the mountain to the East Fork Bridge at the north end of the san Gabriel Reservoir.  Turn right over the bridge onto East Fork Road and continue past the Camp Williams Resort.  It becomes a little tricky when you come to the intersection of the Glendora Mountain Road on your right.  Turn right and immediately turn left crossing over Glendora Road onto Camp Bonita Road.  Do not turn onto Glendora Mountain Road.  Continue to a white bridge and cross it, go up to the end of the road and park in the East Fork Day Use Parking lot. There is a pit toilet there.  If the lot is full, there is plenty of overflow parking back along Camp Bonita Road. 

The Hike:  Hike past the locked gate to Heaton Flat.  At Heaton Flat cross the river for the first of many crossings.  Continue up-river past Swan Rock to the Narrows. The last 1.25 miles to the bridge is unshaded without the benefit of the cooling stream .  Have lunch on the bridge and return to the cars.  Be prepared to make many stream crossings.  HINT:  The road that used to go to the bridge was on the east side.  You can often find portions of a trail over there which cuts down on the number of crossings.  On Hike #1416 we did approximately 12 crossings upstream but approximately 6 downstream by making better use of the east trail/road.  Water level obviously depends on the season.  On Hike #1416, water level was at knee level or less.  Pace is slowed by the number of crossings.  Hike #1416 took 6 hours and 20 mintes including 20 minutes lunch at bridge.

BYO refreshments for trailhead social.
